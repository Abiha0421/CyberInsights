Data Breaches

What is a Data Breach?

A data breach occurs when sensitive, protected, or confidential data is accessed or disclosed without authorization. This could involve the exposure of personal information, financial data, or corporate secrets.

Types of Data Breaches

  • Hacking: Attackers gaining unauthorized access to systems or databases.
  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive data through deceptive communications.
  • Accidental Disclosure: Unintentional sharing of private information through human error or system glitches.
  • Physical Theft: Theft of devices or media containing sensitive data.

How to Protect Yourself from Data Breaches

  • Use strong and unique passwords for each account.
  • Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) wherever possible.
  • Monitor your credit and financial statements regularly.
  • Be cautious when sharing personal information online.
  • Keep your software and security settings up to date.
