Ransomware

What is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a type of malicious software (malware) that locks or encrypts a victim’s data, holding it hostage until a ransom is paid. Attackers often demand payment in cryptocurrency in exchange for the decryption key.

Common Ransomware Types

  • Crypto Ransomware: Encrypts the victim's files and demands payment for the decryption key.
  • Locker Ransomware: Locks the victim out of their system entirely, making it unusable until the ransom is paid.
  • Scareware: Tries to intimidate the victim into paying by claiming their system is infected or compromised, though no actual damage has been done.

How Ransomware Spreads

  • Email attachments or phishing emails with malicious links.
  • Infected software downloads or compromised software updates.
  • Exploiting vulnerabilities in unpatched systems and applications.
  • Through infected USB drives or network connections.

Signs of a Ransomware Attack

  • Inability to access files or programs due to encryption or system lock.
  • Ransom note demanding payment for file decryption or system unlocking.
  • Unusual system behavior or performance degradation.

Preventing Ransomware Attacks

  • Keep all software and systems up to date with the latest security patches.
  • Backup critical data regularly and store it securely offline or in the cloud.
  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account and enable multi-factor authentication (MFA).
  • Be cautious of email attachments and links, especially from unknown senders.
  • Implement endpoint protection and network security tools to detect and block malware.

What to Do If You’re Attacked

  • Disconnect from the internet to prevent further spread of the ransomware.
  • Do not pay the ransom. Paying does not guarantee you will regain access to your files.
  • Report the incident to law enforcement and cybersecurity professionals for assistance.
  • Restore from backups if available, or use decryption tools if provided by trusted security organizations.
